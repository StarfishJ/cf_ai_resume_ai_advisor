## 6. Ownership & Action Verbs (How to describe your work)
*HM/HR Tip: Use these to show high ownership and leadership principles.*
- **High Impact (Use these)**: Designed, Implemented, Optimized, Deployed, Automated, Scaled, Architected, Refactored, Debugged, Improved, Led, Owned, Built, Instrumented
- **Low Impact (Avoid these)**: Participated in, Assisted with, Helped, Learned, Exposed to, Responsible for (passive), Was involved in

## 7. Quantifiable Metrics (Results-Oriented)
*HM/HR Tip: Amazon is a data-driven company. Use these to quantify your results.*
- **Performance**: Latency (ms), Throughput (requests/sec), Response time, Scalability (X users/nodes)
- **Quality**: Test Coverage (%), Bug reduction, Uptime/Reliability (%)
- **Efficiency**: Cost reduction ($/%), CPU/Memory usage optimization, Process automation (hours saved)
- **Scale**: Dataset size (GB/TB), Number of concurrent users, Traffic volume
- **Example Formats**: "Reduced response time by X%", "Processed X msg/s", "Improved test coverage to X%", "Cut cloud cost by X%"

## 8. Amazon Leadership Principles Signals
*HR/HM Tip: HR won't score each LP, but will instinctively look for this "vibe" in your bullets.*
- **Customer Obsession**: Real users, customer impact, user-facing, customer-driven
- **Ownership**: End-to-end, full lifecycle, owned, accountable
- **Bias for Action**: Rapid delivery, shipped, launched, iterated
- **Dive Deep**: Root cause analysis, debugging, performance profiling, deep investigation
- **Invent and Simplify**: Automated, refactored, simplified, reduced complexity
- **Deliver Results**: Quantified outcome, met deadline, delivered on time, measurable impact
